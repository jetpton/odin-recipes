ingredients

  • 1 to 2 steaks (such as ribeye, sirloin, or filet mignon), about 1 inch thick
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil or other high-heat oil
  • 2 tablespoons of butte
  • Optional: minced garlic, fresh herbs (such as thyme or rosemary)

instructions

  1. Take the steaks out of the refrigerator and let them come to room temperature for about 30 minutes. This helps them cook more evenly
  2. Preheat your grill or stovetop skillet over high heat. If using a grill, make sure it is clean and preheated. If using a skillet, choose a heavy-bottomed one that retains heat well.
  3. While the grill or skillet is heating, season the steaks generously with salt and pepper on both sides. You can also add minced garlic or fresh herbs for extra flavor
  4. Drizzle the steaks with the vegetable oil, rubbing it in gently to ensure the steaks are evenly coated
  5. If using a grill, place the steaks on the hot grill grates. If using a skillet, add the steaks directly to the hot skillet. Cook the steaks for about 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare, or adjust the cooking time according to your desired level of doneness.
  6. For a medium-rare steak, aim for an internal temperature of 130-135°F (55-57°C). You can use a meat thermometer to check the temperature. Remember that the steak will continue to cook as it rests.
  7. Just before the steaks are done, add the butter to the skillet or grill, allowing it to melt and coat the steaks. If desired, you can also add a sprig of fresh herbs to the butter for additional flavor.
  8. Remove the steaks from the heat and let them rest for about 5 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, resulting in a more tender and flavorful steak
  9. Slice the steaks against the grain and serve them hot. They pair well with roasted potatoes, steamed vegetables, or a fresh salad
